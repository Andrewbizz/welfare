Priority Groups

Families We Prioritize for Assistance

Assistance is allocated based on urgency and safety risk. The following groups receive priority support. If you or someone you know falls into one of these categories, contact us immediately.

🇺🇸
Highest Priority

U.S. Citizens in Active Threat Zones

Americans residing in regions under Level 3 or Level 4 State Department advisories — including parts of Jalisco, Michoacán, Guerrero, Sinaloa, and Zacatecas — face the most acute risk and receive immediate case intake.

Immediate Evacuation Shelter Coordination
View State Dept. Advisory →
👨‍👩‍👧
Highest Priority

Families with Children Under 18

Households with minors face compounded challenges: disrupted schooling, emotional trauma, and the need to maintain safe living conditions. These families receive priority intake and education continuity assistance.

Education Support Child Welfare Trauma Resources
U.S. Citizen Services →
🏥
Highest Priority

Individuals with Medical Vulnerabilities

U.S. citizens with ongoing medical conditions, limited mobility, or who depend on prescription medications are often the least able to self-evacuate and require coordinated relocation support and medical referrals.

Medical Referrals Mobility Assistance
Emergency Help Abroad →
👴
High Priority

Retirees & Fixed-Income Households

Many American retirees relocated to Mexico for affordability. Displacement — especially sudden job loss or business closure — disproportionately impacts those on Social Security or fixed pensions with limited emergency reserves.

Emergency Housing Food Assistance Relocation Planning
Contact the Embassy →
🪪
High Priority

Dual-National Families

Families holding both U.S. and Mexican citizenship face unique challenges navigating consular systems, documentation requirements, and cross-border family separation when displacement occurs suddenly.

Documentation Help Consular Guidance
Citizen Services →
💻
Standard Priority

Remote Workers & Expats

U.S. citizens who relocated to Mexico as digital nomads or long-term expats may lack the local networks or legal protections needed to navigate a security crisis independently. We help them connect to resources quickly.
