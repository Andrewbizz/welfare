About Us

Who We Are

Safety, Dignity, and a Path Forward

Hope Displaced was founded in response to a crisis that received little mainstream attention: American families — retirees, dual nationals, remote workers, parents with young children — suddenly uprooted by escalating cartel violence across multiple Mexican states, left without housing, income, or a clear way home.

We exist to fill that gap. We are a volunteer-led, community-funded organization that connects displaced U.S. families to emergency housing, essential supplies, consular guidance, and safe travel coordination. We do not replace government services — we bridge the distance between a family in crisis and the systems designed to help them.

Our work is grounded in three principles: act fast, protect privacy, and leave no one behind. Every case we take is treated with urgency and discretion.

What Drives Us

Our Core Values

Everything we do — from the first intake call to the final travel coordination — is guided by these principles.

⚡

Speed in Crisis

When a family contacts us, hours matter. We prioritize rapid case intake, same-day resource referrals, and immediate shelter coordination for families facing active security threats.

🔒

Unconditional Privacy

We never publish names, locations, or case details of the families we serve. Every family's situation is handled with strict discretion to protect their safety and that of those still in the region.

🤝

Dignity First

Displaced families are not statistics. We treat every person with respect, patience, and care — providing structured support without judgment, regardless of how or why they came to be in their situation.

📋

Full Accountability

Every dollar donated is tracked and allocated to a specific program. Every assistance provided is logged. We operate transparently because the families and donors who trust us deserve nothing less.

🌐

Community-Powered

We are funded and staffed by everyday people who believe no American family should be left stranded abroad. Our strength is the network of volunteers, donors, and partner organizations behind us.

🛤️

Long-Term Thinking

Emergency relief is only the beginning. We work with families on relocation planning, document recovery, and safe transition — supporting them not just through the crisis but into a stable future.

Our Story

How Hope Displaced Began

What started as a small network of volunteers sharing resources in a private online group has grown into a coordinated relief organization serving displaced American families across more than a dozen Mexican states. The trigger was simple: too many families were falling through the cracks between overloaded consular offices and underfunded local NGOs.

"We got a call from a retired teacher in Jalisco. She had 48 hours to leave her home of 11 years. No one at the Embassy could pick up. That's when we knew this had to become something bigger."

— Hope Displaced Founder
Early 2024
The Network Forms

A private support group of expats, dual nationals, and concerned family members begins coordinating emergency referrals across social media.

Mid 2024
First Formal Case Intakes

The network handles its first coordinated relocations — three families from Michoacán — establishing the intake and documentation process still used today.

Late 2024
Hope Displaced Launches

The organization officially forms with a volunteer team, a structured aid framework, and its first wave of donor funding to cover emergency housing and travel.

2025–2026
Expanding Reach

Aid programs expand to cover documentation support, education continuity, and mental health referrals. Over 300 families assisted across 12+ affected regions.

Real Impact

Families We've Helped

Behind every case number is a real family. Here are three of the people we've had the privilege to support. Names and identifying details are changed to protect their privacy.

👨‍👩‍👧
The Martinez Family
📍 Jalisco · Assisted Feb 2026

A dual-national family of four — two parents and children aged 6 and 9 — were given 36 hours to vacate their home after cartel activity escalated in their colonia. We coordinated emergency shelter in Guadalajara, covered one week of food assistance, and connected them to the U.S. Consulate for passport renewal for both children.

Emergency Housing Food Assistance Consular Support
👴👵
Retired Couple, Michoacán
📍 Michoacán · Assisted Jan 2026

An American couple in their late 60s who retired to Michoacán 8 years ago found themselves unable to safely leave after road closures and local business shutdowns. We arranged safe transit to Mexico City, connected them to a volunteer host family, and coordinated their return flights to Arizona with medical accommodation for one partner's mobility needs.

Safe Travel Medical Coordination Relocation Support
👩
Single Mother, Guanajuato
📍 Guanajuato · Assisted Dec 2025

A U.S. citizen and single mother of two relocated to Guanajuato for cost of living reasons. When violence spiked in her municipality, her children's school closed indefinitely. We connected her to a remote learning program, provided two weeks of grocery assistance, linked her to a trauma counselor, and helped her begin the process of relocating back to the United States.

Education Continuity Mental Health Food Assistance
